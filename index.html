Correlations Discovered Between Age, Income, and corresponding Health Risks

The D3 enabled interactive scatterplot above allows you to click through multiple variables to compare and contrast their relationships with each other with respect to U.S. states. Click through each variable combination to see how they relate!

Insights:

  • There appears to be a positive correlation between states that lack healtcare and are in poverty. Texas and Louisana seem to be leading with regard to these two measures.
  • States that have higher median incomes tend to habe a low percentage of people who lack healthcare.
  • States with higher rates of smoking tend to have higher impoverished rates. States with higher a higher percentage of smokers tend to have lower median incomes.
  • Across all states, obesity has a positive correlation with poverty rate, no noticable correlation with age, and a negative correlation with median household income.
